Southeast Asia Population Research Summary

Southeast Asia represents a significant portion of the global population, with approximately 690 million people across 11 countries as of 2024. The region includes Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Timor-Leste, and Vietnam.

Key Population Statistics:
- Total ASEAN population (2022): 673.02 million inhabitants
- Global population share: 9%
- Region composition: 11 countries
- Population distribution varies significantly among member states

The region shows diverse demographic characteristics with varying population densities, age structures, and growth rates across different countries. This demographic diversity has important implications for economic development, healthcare systems, and social policies across Southeast Asia.
